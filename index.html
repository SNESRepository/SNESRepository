Welcome!

With digital content being created every second of every day, it’s easy to forget that digital content can be lost though data loss, like hardware crashes or system failures. In order to ensure digital content isn’t lost and it remains accessible, digital content must be carefully curated and preserved. Digital Curation and Preservation is the concept of preserving digital content past its lifecycle through a combination of policies, strategies, and actions to ensure consistent access to digital content over time and prevent data loss. While both terms Digital Curation and Digital Preservation are often used interchangeably to describe the same processes, the term Digital Curation refers to the broader process of not only archiving currently existing digital assets, but also the selection, preservation, maintenance, and collection of said assets. This is in contrast to the term Digital Preservation, which refers to simply maintaining, or preserving, digital information in its original form.

Why is Digital Curation and Preservation Important?

Digital Curation of media allows content that is created digitally to be maintained past its original lifecycle and bypass potential technical risks, such as weathering damage and hard drive failures. With so much digital information being created so frequently, saving and organizing it into clearly defined and accessible places is essential to prevent data loss. The best way to organize digitally curated content is though Metadata, or data that describes data. There are many different types of metadata standards, ranging from descriptive standards that enable the identification and retrieval of information to administrative metadata that contains information about the intellectual property rights of digital information.

Why is Preserving Gaming History Important?

Unfortunately, often times the creators of digital information don’t put thought into ensuring that the content they create will be accessible in the future, and this is very evident in the video game industry. While younger than others, the video game industry has already seen games disappear, never to be rereleased again due to cartridges and other types of physical media they are distributed on being malleable. Even digitally downloadable games that were created in the past decade have disappeared from digital distribution services due to server being taken down, making certain titles completely unplayable for modern audiences. This has left small finite batches of games out in the wild, with limited uses before they’re no longer able to be read by video game consoles. With the decay of video games of all kinds being inevitable, it’s regrettable that most game developers and publishers don’t take pride in preserving their history when they have the opportunity to. For smaller video game developers and publishers that lack the resources to preserve their history, they don’t even have an opportunity.
